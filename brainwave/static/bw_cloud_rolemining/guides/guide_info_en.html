The Brainwave Role Mining feature is based on a mixed approach that combines a top-down approach with a correlation of real and actual rights. This approach maximizes the quality of the roles obtained at the end of the campaign. Role quality is an essential point and depends mainly on three factors:

  • The maximizing of role relevance,
  • The involvement of the project’s participants, such as top management and business application managers, and
  • The quality of the input data, which is very important and must be studied carefully.

As a Role Mining campaign owner, we strongly encourage you to conduct workshops with business managers, organization by organization, so that they can assess the relevance of the roles that the Role Mining service graphically suggests. Only the business manager is able to decide whether or not to split a role in two or add / remove permissions and identities from a role candidate, as only he, alone, knows the true functions of his team.

The campaign creation follows these 4 steps:

  • Campaign information: specify the name, owner (if somebody else should own the campaign) and duration of your Role Mining campaign
  • Scope organizations: select the organization(s) you want to include in the scope of this campaign, based on the organization hierarchy displayed in this section
  • Scope permissions: select the permission(s) you wish to include in the scope of this campaign, based on existing or new permission selection roles
  • Summary: check the details of your campaign's scope and the KPIs related to your scope selection, before finalizing the campaign creation. You can revert back to the previous steps if changes need to be made on the organization or permission selection.
