The selection of permissions depends on the chosen scope of the project. For example, if the roles to be created target the area of finance, then the choice will be made to target the permissions for financial applications.

Reducing the number of permissions is important in order to avoid displaying too much information in the pivot table. This would be detrimental to the business manager's visibility. Applications with a large number of permissions may require an upstream phase of Application Role Mining to create more functional profiles.

It is of the utmost importance to make sure that the permissions are understandable by the business teams. The presence of a description is a good indicator. If the business lines do not understand the meaning of the permissions, role creation will be very difficult or, perhaps, impossible. The quality of the documentation describing permissions should be assessed with the client in mind. Cryptic permission codes are not necessarily problematic if they are commonly handled by the business lines.

Permissions can be documented during the workshops, but best practice is to document them prior to the workshops, for two reasons:

  • Documenting permissions should be done with the application manager and not the business manager.
  • Because the time a business manager spends in a workshop is valuable, it should not be spent trying to understand the meaning of permissions. For the session to be effective, the business manager must be able to focus on the roles.
