The following activities can be performed from this dashboard:

  • Identification of privileged accounts.
    The accounts loaded in the Brainwave data cube can belong to business applications as well as to IT resources such as servers, databases or network elements. Among these accounts (technical, user or orphaned) some have privileges giving access to extended permissions or are members of administrator groups.
    PAM Booster helps identify privileged accounts by automatically or manually setting sensitivity to permissions or groups. It is possible to designate privileged accounts in a discretionary manner, but it is preferable to create rules that allow these accounts to be detected on an ongoing basis.

  • Creating and prioritizing account on-boarding scopes in CyberArk.
    With regards to the privileged accounts, it is necessary to choose the prioritization and scheduling of the on-boarding into CyberArk.
    It is possible to set up scopes using PAM Booster in order to do this.

  • Risk monitoring linked to the quality of the CyberArk configuration.
    Several sets of controls are available on privileged accounts not yet on-boarded into CyberArk, the configuration of safes and vaults as well as the rights managed through CyberArk or Active Directory groups.
    KPIs summarize the situation and are accompanied by trends to track the evolution of these controls over time.
