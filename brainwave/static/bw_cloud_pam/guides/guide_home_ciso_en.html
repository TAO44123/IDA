Governance Dashboard

The colored tiles display KPIs and trends risks linked to privileged accounts.

The background color of the tiles reflects the trend from the situation in the comparison analysis period displayed on the right.  Green  is for improvement,  Red  is for worsening, and  Blue  indicates a stable situation.

The tiles show the configuration risks in CyberArk with a focus on credentials that do not have password rotation (CPM) enabled, as this puts the target resources at risk.

On all tiles, it is possible to click on the KPI or the percentage to go to a details page that displays the list.

The tiles are detailed below:

  • Credentials without password rotation: Password rotation should be enabled whenever possible to ensure the maximum security and confidentiality of credentials. When this is not possible, there must be an associated justification. This KPI displays the percentage of credentials without password rotation and without justification, that are not members of a safe with CPM disabled.
  • Control defects linked to the security of the CyberArk accesses: This KPI is the number of security policy errors of users with access to CyberArk. Only errors with a high or very high level of risk are considered in this KPI (risk level 3 and 4). They relate to users of vaults and safes with access to credentials or with administrative or maintenance rights to the CyberArk configuration. It is important to correct these errors to limit security risks and to comply with the security policy.
  • Control defects linked to the quality of the CyberArk accesses: This KPI presents the number of errors related to the quality policy of the access to CyberArk. These errors concern the users of vaults and safes who have access to confidential credentials or who have administration or maintenance rights on the CyberArk configuration. It is important to correct these errors to improve the maintenance and optimization of the CyberArk configuration.
  • Accounts for which the owner has departed with access to CyberArk safes: This KPI presents the number of user accounts having access to at least one CyberArk safe and owned by an identity having left the company.
